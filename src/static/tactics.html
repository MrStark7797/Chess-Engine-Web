Tactics

Tactics in chess are vital, used to help bring an advantage to a player.
Through a series of moves, tactics are played to for many reasons like: Having a strong opening (start to a game), winning material or even helping close the game in checkmate!

Openings

Sicilian Defense

The Sicilian Defense is the most popular response to White's 1.e4.
One of the oldest registered openings, the Sicilian is full of theory and was used by most of the greatest players in history.

Credit chess.com/openings/Sicilian-Defense

There are many variations of the Sicilian inluding Closed & Open:
Closed - white chooses to closes of the position in order to expand the kingside

Credit chess.com/openings/Sicilian-Defense

Open Sicilian, however, is the most common way to meet the sicilian for white leading to more open and tactical games
The common lines are 1.e4 c5 2.Nf3 d6 3.d4 cxd4 4.Nxd4 or 1.e4 c5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.d4

Credit chess.com/openings/Sicilian-Defense

Italian game

The Italian game is one of the oldest openings, leading with e4.
e4 can lead to slower and more postional games or more open and tactical battles.
Easliy learnt by beginers, when developed and mastered, the Italian game is part of the repertoire of players of every level!

Credit chess.com/openings/Italian-Game

The Italian game leads to many variations ie the Guioco Piano and Pianissimo
Giuoco Piano - While its name translates to soft/slow game, despite its name white can force a more open game with 5.d4

Credit chess.com/openings/Italian-Game

Giuoco Pianissimo - As the name suggests leads to a very slow gamewith a d3 push with either 4.c3 and 5.d3 or 4.d3 creating a more closed and positional game

Credit chess.com/openings/Italian-Game
